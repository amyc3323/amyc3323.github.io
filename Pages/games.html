Pizzapocalypse (2023)

Role: Producer, artist, animator
Tools: Unity, Aesprite, Autodesk Maya

What is the most logical course of action to take in the middle of a zombie apocalypse? Well obviously, you order a fresh hot pizza! In Pizzapocalypse, you play as a pizza delivery driver, swerving around zombies and crashing into obstacles to quickly deliver pizzas to your eagerly awaiting customers. The zombies? Well of course, they're trying to steal your delicious pizza! Deliver pizzas to increase your restaurant’s rating and collect ingredients to cook even more delicious pizzas.

This game was made in 3 weeks during the National High School Game Academy program in 2023. Our final presentation presenting Pizzapocalypse can be watched on the left. It's also displayed on the official CMU NHSGA website! I worked with Dianne Cao, Ethan Kellogg, Ethan Bai, and Henry Shen as the producer and one of two artists. My main art contributions were the pizza delivery driver (all 16 directions of him!), the street tilemap, and all animations, but as the producer, I also scheduled all group meetings, planned the timeline, assigned tasks, and managed the team.

After the program, we went on to publish the game to the Apple appstore, so please check out Pizzapocalypse! on the App store.

Tea Time in 5 (2025)

Role: Producer, UI artist & designer, 3D artist, programmer, level designer
Tools: Unity, Procreate, Autodesk Maya

What are you willing to risk in the pursuit of beauty? It’s tea time in 5 minutes and you’re a plain piece of cookie dough on the bottom shelf of the tea tray. But everyone knows that the prettiest pastries are located at the very top. Roll around and parkour to reach the top of the tray in time for the tea party and collect decorations along the way to become the prettiest. But beware, ecause beauty comes with a price: each decoration adds to your hitbox, making movement more and more difficult. Will you make it in time for tea?

This game was made in 1 week for the Brackeys Game Jam 2025.2. I worked with Dianne Cao, Ethan Kellogg, Ethan Bai, and Henry Shen (Team Pineapple again!) as the producer and one of two artists. However, I also contributed heavily to coding the game and laid out the game scene and UI. My main contributions were organizing the team, modelling and texturing the tea party sandwiches and tray, and coding the game flow and high score system.

Overworked (2025)

Role: UI artist, programmer
Tools: Unity, Procreate

During NHSGA in 2025, the TAs decided to try and develop a game in 3 weeks alongside the students. Thus, we came up with an overcooked-style game where you play as us, the TAs, rushing around a modified version of the Entertainment Technology Center while trying to “solve” student issues through various hyperbolized minigames.

This game is a networked multiplayer game made by all fourteen NHSGA TAs. I organized the game document, drew the character reference sheet for the models of each TA, and also drew and laid out some of the simpler UI. I also drew the logo! For programming, my main contribution was creating the Unity animator minigame where the player is tasked with finding missing transitions in a replica of Unity's animator in order to solve the minigame.

Roos (2023)

Role: Producer, artist
Tools: Unity, Aesprite

In an Australian wildfire, team up with a cockatoo to fight for the last safe tree in a wildfire. Kick down the enemy robot kangaroos, dodge snipers and boomerangs, and claim the tree as your own. Survive for as long as possible as more enemies swarm around you. This game is based on the arcade game Jouse and tries to replicate the mechanics and feel of the original game while adding a new theme and gameplay mechanics.

As a “trial run” for NHSGA, students first go through a lightning round where they are put into a team and given one week to recreate a classic arcade game but with a new stylistic theme. I worked with Finn Murphy, Kishi Wijaya and Katherine Thesling as a solo artist and producer. We were assigned the game Joust and wanted to preserve its original goofy chaos. After a bit of brainstorming, we settled on the idea of boxing kangaroos and the idea just took off. I drew all of the sprites and animations in the game, including the intro cutscene.

Brick Breaker (2025)

Role: Programmer
Tools: Unity

As someone who did not know how to code until 11th grade, I was really excited to be brought back to NHSGA as a programming TA for the beginning programming class. As a part of this job, not only did I get to teach a few of the classes, but I got to help develop the c urriculum with both beginner and non-programmers in mind. We decided that a good way of teaching both C# and Unity was to walk the students through building a very simple game. This style would provide structure and an understanding of what was expected, but allowed for full creativity. Plus students would walk away at the end with a finished product.

While building this game, we taught students many topics ranging from maneuvering around in Unity and colliders to singletones and coroutines. Working with an ETC faculty member and another TA, I helped to design and program the game while creating lesson plans to teach the students. Though the game isn’t the most advanced, a lot of thought went into how it could be created in a beginner-friendly way.

Threadbound (WIP)

Role: Solo developer
Tools: Unity, Aesprite

Threadbound is an ongoing project of mine based on a short story I wrote in 2024. The story takes place on a waterlogged planet where land is rare and storms are frequent. In this world, there is a myth that if you throw a bottle into the sea with a keepsake of a lost loved one, they will be able to find their way home. The protagonist, Amaya, has the magical ability to see threads in bottles that link people together. She sets out to try and reunite these people, all while searching for her brother along the way.

This game was started as both a way for me to get more familiar with Unity, but also as a game design exercise to see how I can translate the elements of my world into an interactive game. Unfortunately, I’ve been stuck on how to make the sailing feel good in the game and haven’t proceeded. I am currently debating on starting over in 3D.
