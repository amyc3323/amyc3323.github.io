About Me

If I had to describe myself in two words, it would be "creative" and "logical". I think this is quite interesting, because at first, logic and creativity seem like opposites—like oil and water. Logic is confined in strict rules while creativity allows for unlimited possibilities. And for a lot of my life, these qualities of mine have been kept separate. From a young age, I've loved to paint and draw, and every year I seem to find a new creative hobby (like writing or music) that captures my interests for a few months before I inevitably return to art. On the flip side, I’ve also always loved solving logic puzzles and figuring out math problems.

It wasn’t until high school that I realized that these interests could mesh in the form of computer science, and I fell in love with the way that simple lines of text are able to build powerful programs that can change the world. I realized that I was wrong: logic and creativity are not opposites, but instead complement each other to enable innovation. This realization inspired me to pursue computer science at Carnegie Mellon. Now, I enjoy combining all my skills in the form of video game development. I love constructing the underlying logic behind the game and watching as that logic brings my art to life. Because of this, it should come as no surprise that my hobbies involve reading fantasy books, drawing and 3D modeling, and developing games. Some pieces of media that especially inspire me are Six of Crows by Leigh Bardugo, Epic the Musical by Jorge Rivera-Herrans, and Big Fish and Begonia (2016).

Art

Art has been a part of my life for as long as I can remember through many different mediums, and I can remember attending art class as early as when I was six. Though I’ve experimented with acrylics, oil pastels, graphite, charcoal, and more, today, I enjoy oil painting, 3D modeling, digital drawing, and animation. One thing I find particularly interesting is how well all my interests and hobbies fit together. For example, I love writing which helps me design characters. Additionally, a lot of the art I’ve done recently has been for game development projects.

Just like with coding, I think the most appealing part of art to me is its unlimited potential: animation makes my drawings come to life, 3D modeling turns my ideas three-dimensional, paintings can capture a space and a feeling. Additionally, I love how art allows me to engage with the content I enjoy. Undertale was the game that inspired me to teach myself digital art, and many other games and movies have inspired me to continue since then.

Beyond that, art has taught me many valuable lessons. Just like how perspective distortion needs to be considered when trying to make a drawing feel solid and three-dimensional, art taught me to look at problems from different perspectives to form my own conclusions. Drawing also allows me to express myself and take time to enjoy my own presence. Overall, while I do enjoy making pretty things, art is more than just that to me: it’s part of my identity and something I hope to never let go.

To see examples of my art, go to my art portfolio!

Coding

Even though I attend CMU for CS, I didn’t actually learn to code until sophomore year of highschool when I took APCSA. However, I quickly realized that while computer science lends itself to my logical side with its rules and syntax that need to be assembled in particular ways to achieve the desired outcome, it’s also very creative. While coding has its own rules, you can employ the syntax in many creative ways to solve the same problem.

Overall, I like how this field is a mix of both creativity and logic, reflecting my own strengths and interests, and I like how computer science is being used to creatively change the world by helping people in different ways. During my junior year of high school, I worked on three assistive technology projects that taught me the power of computer science and how it can be used to create devices and apps that solve specific challenges for specific people. Finally, CS is also vast and limitless, just like art. There’s UI/UX, algorithms, mobile app development, video game development, and more. I’ve always enjoyed learning a variety of different skills and so I like going into a field where there are many options to choose from and pursue.

Games

I’ve always enjoyed playing video games. They can be fun and stress relieving, but also serve as a way for me to spend time and connect with my friends. However, game development has been the most recent addition to my repertoire of interests. Game development is appealing to me because it combines all of my interests—writing, art, and coding—and I love designing interactive worlds and stories.

In 2023, I attended CMU’s National High School Game Academy (NHSGA) precollege program as an artist. During the six-week program, I learned a lot about Unity and the 3D art pipeline for game asset creation. I also had the opportunity to work in two teams on two game projects as a producer and artist, and one game was even published to the app store! It was an incredible experience that gave me the tools to not only build games, but work in interdisciplinary teams on tight deadlines.

Because of this, I was thrilled to come back in 2025 as a programming and production TA where I helped teach classes and mentor students. Specifically, I helped develop the introductory programming curriculum and served as a liaison between team producers and the TAs. Beyond that, I loved helping younger students pursue their passions, and seeing the quality of their games really amazed me. NHSGA really cemented a love for game development, and I hope to someday work for a game studio!
